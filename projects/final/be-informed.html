Spay & Neuter, Adoption, and Fostering Awareness

Learn about the importance of spaying and neutering, adoption, and fostering. These efforts help reduce the stray cat population, provide better lives for cats in need, and contribute to a healthier community.

What is Kitten Season?

Kitten Season is the time of year when free-roaming cats begin to give birth to multiple litters of kittens. In many places, this occurs from spring to early winter, but Hawaii's warm climate allows Kitten Season to extend nearly year-round.

During this time, the Hawaiian Humane Society and other animal shelters see a huge increase in the number of kittens and mother cats needing care.

Did you know?

According to the Hawaii Humane Society:

  • Kitten Season is when female cats are most likely to go into heat and give birth to multiple litters.
  • Cats can go into heat as early as four months old.
  • A single female cat can give birth to over 100 kittens during her lifetime!

The Importance of Spaying & Neutering

Spaying and neutering help control the cat population and reduce the number of strays. These procedures also lower health risks like infections and certain cancers.

Did you know?
A single pair of unspayed cats and their offspring can produce thousands of kittens in just a few years.

Adoption vs. Buying Pets

Adopting cats from shelters saves lives and reduces overpopulation. Many shelter cats are healthy and loving but are overlooked due to common myths.

Buying pets supports breeders, while adoption offers a second chance to cats in need.

Fostering Cats

Fostering is temporary care for cats until they find permanent homes. It helps shelters reduce overcrowding and gives cats a safe, loving environment.

Want to foster?
Reach out to local shelters to learn how you can start making a difference by becoming a foster parent.
