Alphea Podador | An Independent Project Dedicated to Cat Welfare

Final Project Proposal

Title and Topic of My Site:

  • Title: "Cat Advocacy Hub"
  • Topic: The website will focus on raising awareness about cats, showcasing my personal stories of my cats from the Philippines and the BYU-Hawaii campus, and promoting adoption, fostering, and the importance of spaying and neutering. It will also serve as a resource hub for local cat advocacy organizations.
  • Why I Chose This Topic: I chose this topic because of my deep love for cats and my desire to advocate for a better environment for them. Cats hold a special place in my heart, and through this website, I hope to educate others on the importance of adopting, fostering, and supporting organizations that help cats in our communities. One of my personal goals is to feed stray cats in the area, and I hope to encourage others to get involved as well. The website will also serve as a platform for sharing personal stories and information about cats, making it relatable to other cat lovers.

Content on Each Page:

  1. Home Page:
    • Introduction about myself, my love for cats, and my advocacy for creating a better environment for them.
    • A brief overview of the site and how it serves as a resource for cat lovers, advocates, and anyone interested in helping cats.
    • I will include my goal to feed stray cats and encourage visitors to join me in this effort.
  2. Page About My Cats (Philippines):
    • Images, names, and stories of the four cats I have in the Philippines organized in a table with columns.
    • Each cat’s personality, how I grew up with them, and any unique experiences I’ve had with them.
    • Personal photographs I took.
  3. Page About Cats on BYU-Hawaii Campus:
    • Table showcasing pictures, names, and locations of the cats I have observed on the BYU-Hawaii campus.
    • Personal photographs I took of the cats around campus.
    • Table with columns for:
      • Image
      • Name
      • Location (where they are commonly found on campus)
  4. Spay & Neuter, Adoption, and Fostering Awareness Page:
    • Information about the importance of spaying and neutering cats.
    • Why it’s better to adopt than shop for cats, and the benefits of fostering.
    • Simple, engaging content to educate people on how they can help cats in their communities.
  5. Resources Page:
    • Information and links to cat advocacy organizations such as BYU-Hawaii Cat Club, Hawaiian Humane Society, Hawaii Cat Foundation, and others.
    • Acknowledgment that the website is an independent project and not affiliated with these organizations, but aims to support them.
    • References to reputable organizations like:
      • Cats Club - Brigham Young University–Hawaii
      • Hawaiian Humane Society
      • Lanai Cat Sanctuary
      • Popoki Place Oahu Cat Sanctuary
      • Hawaii Happy Cats
      • KAT Charities
  6. Contact Form Page (with Processing):
    • Fields will include:
      • Name
      • Email
      • Message (with a description of what the person is inquiring about or sharing).
      • Checkbox: “I’m interested in learning more about these organizations, adopting a cat or getting involved in local cat advocacy. I would also like to join the effort to feed stray cats in the community.”
    • Data from the form will be sent through AJAX to display a confirmation message without refreshing the page.

How I Will Collect Information/Images/Content:

  • Images: I will use personal photos of my cats and photos I took of the BYU-Hawaii campus cats.
  • Content: Information about the importance of spaying, neutering, adoption, and fostering will come from research and reputable sources like the Hawaiian Humane Society. For the organizations, I’ll gather information from their official websites and social media accounts, ensuring to reference them properly.
